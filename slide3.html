Data Analysis with SQL

Data analysis involves querying databases to uncover patterns, trends, and insights that drive decision-making. SQL enables efficient analysis of large datasets, helping businesses optimize operations and strategies.

Why is Data Analysis Important?

  • Informed Decisions: Identify high-performing products or customer segments to focus resources effectively.
  • Efficiency: Quickly process and summarize large datasets to reveal actionable insights.

Use Case 1: Retail Inventory Optimization

A retailer uses SQL to analyze sales data, identifying which products sell best in specific seasons or regions. For example, querying sales by category can reveal that Electronics sell more in Q4, guiding inventory stocking decisions. 

SELECT Category, SUM(Sales_Amount) as Total_Sales
FROM Sales
WHERE Sale_Date LIKE '2025-Q4%'
GROUP BY Category;

Use Case 2: Customer Segmentation

A business segments customers by purchase behavior, using SQL to group customers by total spending. This helps target marketing campaigns, such as offering discounts to high-value customers. 

SELECT Customer_ID, SUM(Sales_Amount) as Total_Spent
FROM Sales
GROUP BY Customer_ID
HAVING SUM(Sales_Amount) > 1000;
